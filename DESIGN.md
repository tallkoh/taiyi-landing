## Problem Statement

Middle-class working adults of Chinese heritage in SEA (and the global Chinese diaspora) take bazi/qimen/fengshui seriously and pay for it today — but the market is bifurcated:

1. **High end (RM5,000+):** Personal consultations with brand-name masters (e.g. Joey Yap). Inaccessible to most.
2. **Low end (free):** Apps like Cece, BaziAI, and raw DeepSeek prompts. Generic, no calendrical depth, "read once and done" engagement, no human credibility.

There is no product that delivers Joey Yap-quality personalized bazi + qimen analysis (depth, cultural authority, calendrical localization) at SaaS pricing for either consumers OR the working practitioners who serve them. The founder has a competitive asset: a curated qimen source library that most existing apps lack.

## Demand Evidence

**What we have:**
- Founder's mother/aunt currently pays for fengshui services (~S$300-800/year range). This is real but family-bias-confounded — they buy out of love as much as need.
- Reference market: Joey Yap's seminar/book ecosystem proves white-collar SEA Chinese spend money on bazi content. His personal consultations are price-gated, leaving demand unmet.
- Chinese-mainland market data (research): Cece (Tencent-backed) at 1M+ MAU, 2M+ WeChat posts in Feb 2025 about people using DeepSeek directly for bazi readings. Category demand is enormous globally.

**What we DO NOT have:**
- Zero customer-discovery interviews with non-family target users
- No working bazi consultant has agreed to use a prototype
- No demand test (landing page, paid ad, waitlist conversion) has been run

This is a pre-product idea. The honest demand evidence is: the *category* is proven; the *specific product-market fit* is not.

## Status Quo

What the target customer (Jessica, 42, KL/SG finance manager — Joey Yap seminar-goer) does today:

- Buys Joey Yap books and attends his RM200-800 seminars (knowledge, no personalization)
- Cannot afford Joey Yap's RM5,000+ personal consultation
- Uses free fortune apps (low quality, no qimen depth) sporadically
- Asks family/friends for master referrals when there's a major life event (house, wedding, business)
- Cash/red-packet payment culture for in-person services

What working bazi consultants (Approach B target) do today:

- Hand-draw or use desktop tools to generate charts
- Write reports manually in Word/Pages
- Maintain client lists in spreadsheets or WeChat groups
- The only purpose-built SaaS is FST Feng Shui Tools at SGD $149/yr (chart generation only, no AI)

## Target User & Narrowest Wedge

**Primary (long-term, Approach B):** Working bazi/fengshui consultants in SEA + overseas Chinese diaspora. Estimated 5-10K practitioners. They charge clients S$200-3,000 per consultation. They are time-poor (chart prep + report writing eats 60-70% of paid time). Willingness to pay for a tool that lets them serve 2-3x more clients with the same hours: high.

**Secondary (parallel validation, Approach A-lite):** Jessica, 42, white-collar SEA Chinese / overseas Chinese diaspora. Reads bazi books, attends seminars, owns 2-3 Joey-Yap-tier books. Will pay $9-15/mo for daily/weekly personalized content if delivery format (push, email, app) matches her habits.

**Narrowest wedge:** A weekly personalized fengshui newsletter (geo + weather + qimen calendar + her bazi pillar) for $12/mo. One product, one channel (email + simple webapp), one ad funnel. This is what we'd ship in week 6 to validate consumer demand while doing B2B research.

## Constraints

- Founder is solo (assumption — confirm)
- No existing audience or distribution channel
- No engineering team — must be built with AI-leveraged single-developer velocity
- Geographic playable surface: SEA diaspora (SG, MY, ID, TH) + overseas Chinese (UK, US, AU). **Mainland China is OFF-LIMITS** due to April 2025 CAC crackdown on AI fortune-telling.
- Founder's edge: curated qimen source materials. This is the moat for both tracks.

## Premises (locked in Phase 3)

1. **Persona:** Target is "Jessica, 42, Joey Yap seminar-goer," NOT Auntie Mei the believer-housewife and NOT random millennial astrology browser.
2. **Pricing:** $38/mo is unsupportable for self-serve AI-only. Market ceiling for consumer is $9-12/mo unless humans are in the loop. B2B can support $79+/mo.
3. **Differentiation:** "AI does my bazi" is no longer a moat (DeepSeek does it free). Real moat = cultural authority + calendrical localization (qimen library) + human-in-loop (for premium tiers) + B2B workflow lock-in.
4. **Distribution:** This is the hardest problem, not the product. Without a distribution wedge, the business dies on arrival.
5. **Market:** Mainland China off-limits. SEA diaspora + overseas Chinese is the playable surface.

## Cross-Model Perspective

Landscape research (Sonnet 4.6 subagent, web-grounded):

- **Joey Yap has no consumer AI reading product** — chart tools, courses, seminars only. White space exists but he could close it any quarter.
- **Mainland market commoditized.** Cece (Tencent) at 1M+ MAU. "DeepSeek fortune-telling" was a 2M-post viral phenomenon on WeChat in Feb 2025. The core "AI reading" product has near-zero differentiation in the Mandarin world.
- **Price ceiling for self-serve digital fortune is $9-12/mo** across Co-Star, CHANI, Nebula, AstroTalk. $38/mo would have to compete with live-practitioner marketplaces — not feasible without humans.
- **Practitioner SaaS gap is real.** FST Feng Shui Tools (SGD $149/yr) is the only identified competitor. No AI-assisted interpretation tool exists. Clearer WTP, lower churn, smaller TAM.
- **The "read once" churn trap is the central business problem in this category.** A natal chart never changes; retention must come from calendrical content (weekly fengshui, annual luck pillars, flying-star monthly updates).
- **No SEA-native English-language Chinese-metaphysics subscription product exists at scale.** Genuine white space — but white space ≠ validated demand.

## Approaches Considered

### Approach A: Consumer SaaS at market price ("Cosmos for the diaspora")
$12/mo mobile-first app. Bazi natal chart on signup, weekly localized fengshui push, annual luck-pillar updates. Big TAM, hard unit economics, brutal distribution, high churn. Effort: M. Risk: High.

### Approach B: B2B SaaS for fengshui practitioners ("Bloomberg for bazi consultants")
$79/mo browser-based tool. AI drafts natal + qimen + flying-star + date-selection reports; practitioner reviews/edits/exports as branded PDF. (Client CRM and re-consultation reminders are post-MVP.) Higher WTP, lower churn, smaller TAM (estimated 5-10K seats in SEA + overseas Chinese diaspora — best-guess figure, not sourced; needs validation via Track 1 research). At $79/mo × ~2,000 seats = ~$1.9M ARR realistic mid-term; ceiling ~$5-10M if international diaspora is reachable. Effort: M-L. Risk: Medium.

### Approach C: Hybrid marketplace ("AstroTalk for Chinese metaphysics")
$49/reading + $19/mo. AI drafts 70%, credentialed master reviews + signs. Recurring + transactional. Two-sided marketplace. Effort: L. Risk: High.

## Recommended Approach

**Two-track parallel: B (long-term) + A-lite (validation experiment).**

### Week 1 blockers (must resolve before either track ships content)

**B1 — Qimen library IP audit.** Before any qimen content is generated for a paying user or even a free newsletter, confirm every source in your index is public-domain (奇門遁甲統宗, 烟波釣叟賦, 紫白訣 and other classical texts published before 1929 are safe in most jurisdictions). Modern translations, scanned books from contemporary masters, and Joey-Yap-tier publications are NOT safe. If even 20% of your library turns out to be copyrighted modern material, the moat collapses and you need to rebuild. This is a Day 1 task, not an open question.

**B2 — PDPA / PDPL compliance posture.** You will collect date-of-birth and birth-time (sensitive data under Malaysia PDPA 2010 and Singapore PDPA 2012). Before the landing page accepts a single email, you need: (a) a privacy policy stating purpose of collection, retention period, and deletion mechanism; (b) explicit consent checkbox on signup; (c) a delete-my-data endpoint; (d) Singapore DNC registry check if SMS is in scope (it isn't yet, fine). Hire a lawyer or use a SG/MY-aware template (e.g. Termly, iubenda with SG/MY toggle). Budget S$500-1,500 for a one-shot review.

### Track 1 — B2B (Approach B), weeks 1-12:
**Goal:** Find 3-5 working bazi/fengshui consultants in SEA willing to be design partners. No product yet — just research and outreach.

- **Weeks 1-4:** Map the SEA practitioner ecosystem. Use LinkedIn, IFSA (International Feng Shui Association) member directories, Facebook groups, master websites, Joey Yap alumni lists. (Xiaohongshu is a mainland CN platform with limited SEA reach — defer unless Track 1 interviews show MY/SG practitioners actually use it for work.) Build a spreadsheet of 100+ practitioners with contact info, estimated client volume, current tools, pricing tier.
- **Weeks 3-6:** Interview 10 practitioners. Questions: what eats your time? How do you generate reports today? Would AI-drafted client analyses speed you up if YOU got final review/edit? What would you pay for this? **Contingency:** if cold-outreach response rate produces fewer than 3 interview commits by day 5 of outreach, switch to paid recruitment via User Interviews or Respondent.io (~US$80-120/session for verified practitioners). Budget US$1,000 for this fallback.
- **Weeks 6-10:** Recruit 3-5 as design partners. "Design partner" is defined operationally as: **a signed email agreement to (a) use the prototype within 7 days of receiving credentials, (b) provide feedback within 48 hours of each sprint demo, (c) give a quotable testimonial if value is delivered.** Free use for 6 months in exchange. **Defensive milestone:** by end of week 10 you need 3 signed agreements — this is the practitioner workflow lock-in that defends against a Joey Yap competitive response if he launches a clone in months 6-12.
- **Weeks 10-12:** Build the practitioner MVP — chart engine + AI draft + plain HTML report export. Ship to design partners. Iterate. **Branded PDF export, CRM, and client-tracking are post-MVP (weeks 14+).** Do not gold-plate the first ship.

### Track 2 — Consumer validation (Approach A-lite), weeks 1-8:
**Goal:** Spend US$3,000-4,000 on Meta/IG ads to test whether Jessica-types convert on a $12/mo personalized fengshui newsletter. **Launch in English only.** Traditional Chinese localization is a Track 2 follow-up if and only if signup analytics show >30% of email signups come from non-English-dominant addresses or self-identify in onboarding.

- **Weeks 1-2:** Build a one-page landing site. Headline: "Your personal weekly fengshui — based on your bazi, where you live, and the qimen calendar." Above the fold: email capture + free bazi snapshot. Below the fold: $12/mo subscription pitch with sample content. Tech stack: Next.js or Astro on Vercel/Cloudflare Pages, Resend for email, Stripe for payments. Built with Cursor or Claude Code (single-developer AI-assisted velocity assumption: ~3-5 working days for landing page + payment integration).
- **Weeks 2-4:** Build the newsletter engine. AI-generated weekly content combining (a) user's bazi pillar, (b) device geolocation, (c) weather API, (d) current qimen calendar pillar from your library. Send via Beehiiv or a Resend + simple Postgres stack. AI-assisted dev budget: ~5-7 working days.
- **Weeks 4-8:** Run Meta/IG ad tests over a real 4-week active acquisition window. Target: SEA Chinese 30-50, interests = Joey Yap, Lillian Too, fengshui, bazi, Chinese astrology. Measure: CPM, CPC, landing-page conversion, email-to-paid conversion, week-2 retention.
- **Decision gate week 8:** If CAC < $40 (revised — was unrealistically tight at $30 against $2K budget) and 30-day retention > 50%, double down on consumer. If not, the data still tells you what content/positioning resonates, which informs Track 1's pitch to practitioners.

### Funnel math (so we're not lying to ourselves)

At SEA Meta CPM ~$5 and 4 weeks of US$3,500 spend = 700,000 impressions. At 1.5% CTR = 10,500 clicks. At 5-8% landing-to-email = **525-840 email signups**. At a realistic cold-traffic email-to-paid conversion of 2% (NOT 6%) for a $12/mo subscription in a trust-sensitive category = **10-17 paid subscribers** by week 8. Total acquisition cost per paid sub: ~$200-350.

**Read this honestly:** at these realistic numbers, Track 2's purpose is NOT to be profitable. It is to (a) test whether the demand signal exists at all, (b) generate a 500-signup email list you can later convert at higher rates through retention nurture, and (c) produce content/positioning data that feeds Track 1's B2B pitch. If you see 30+ paid subs at week 8 with $3.5K spend, that's a green light. If you see 10-17, that's normal-and-not-dead, lean in on retention. If you see <5, the consumer angle is probably broken and you go all-in on Track 1.

### Kill switches (added to prevent founder burnout / sunk-cost spiral)

- **Week 4 stall check:** if Track 1 has zero interview commits AND Track 2's landing page isn't live, stop both and reassess scope. The 12-week plan assumes both tracks compound; if neither has started, the plan is invalid.
- **Week 8 hard gate:** if Track 2 fails (<5 paid AND <300 email signups) AND Track 1 has fewer than 5 completed interviews, pause for one week of pure reflection. Don't escalate ad spend or push harder. Re-examine premises.

**Why this works:** Track 1 has the better unit economics and clearer distribution path (you can email every prospect personally). Track 2 is a cheap fast experiment that produces signal AND content (the newsletter posts themselves become marketing for Track 1 — practitioners will see them). They share infra: same qimen content engine, same bazi calculation library, same brand.

**What this is NOT:** This is not "build everything in parallel." Track 2 is intentionally tiny — a landing page + a newsletter + ads. Track 1 is research-heavy in months 1-2 (zero code), then prototype-light in month 3. Total dev workload is one solo founder using AI for ~3 months to ship both.
